Arctic Squirrel

When the arctic ground squirrel hibernates its body temperature drops to 26.8 degrees Fahrenheit. During hibernation, every two or three weeks, the arctic ground squirrel will shiver itself back to its normal body temperature of 97.5 degrees Fahrenheit and won't get cold again for another 12 to 15 hours. This allows the squirrel's brain to survive without getting damaged. When the squirrel emerges from hibernation its brain returns to normal within about two hours. Some research suggests that its brain works even better after hibernation.
